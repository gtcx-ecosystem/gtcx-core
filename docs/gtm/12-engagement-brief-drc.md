# Engagement Brief — Democratic Republic of Congo

**Approach:** Direct engagement (no formal sandbox program)
**Entry point:** Ministry of Mines + international traceability partners
**Primary commodity:** 3TG minerals (tin, tantalum, tungsten, gold)

---

## Context

The DRC has no formal fintech regulatory sandbox. However, the 3TG mineral verification opportunity is the largest by international regulatory impact:

- **EU Conflict Minerals Regulation** (2021/821) requires importers to verify responsible sourcing
- **US Dodd-Frank Act Section 1502** requires conflict mineral disclosure
- **OECD Due Diligence Guidance** provides the framework all parties reference
- **ITSCI** (ITRI Tin Supply Chain Initiative) is the dominant traceability system — but paper-based and facing credibility challenges

GTCX's cryptographic verification protocol provides the digital layer that international regulation increasingly demands.

## Engagement Strategy

### Phase 1: International Partner Alignment (Month 1-2)

| Partner                                            | Role                                       | Approach                                                                                         |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **ITSCI** (ITRI)                                   | Dominant traceability system for 3TG       | Position GTCX as digital complement to ITSCI's physical tagging. Not a replacement — an upgrade. |
| **Responsible Minerals Initiative (RMI)**          | Industry coalition (Apple, Intel, Samsung) | Present at RMI conference or working group. Downstream buyers want better verification.          |
| **BGR** (German geological survey)                 | Technical assistance in DRC mining         | BGR operates CTC (Certified Trading Chains) program. GTCX provides the cryptographic layer.      |
| **IPIS** (International Peace Information Service) | ASM mapping and monitoring                 | Data partnership — IPIS has the maps, GTCX has the verification protocol.                        |

### Phase 2: Ministry of Mines Engagement (Month 2-3)

**Key contacts:**

- Direction des Mines (mining directorate)
- SAEMAPE (Service d'Assistance et d'Encadrement du Small-Scale Mining)
- CEEC (Centre d'Expertise, d'Evaluation et de Certification)

**Talking points:**

1. GTCX enables digital compliance with EU Conflict Minerals Regulation — reduces audit burden on Congolese exporters
2. Offline-first design works in Kivu, Katanga, and Ituri provinces without reliable connectivity
3. Identity credentials for ASM cooperatives formalize operators without requiring smartphone apps or internet
4. Zero-knowledge proofs allow proving compliance without revealing commercially sensitive production data to competitors

### Phase 3: Pilot Scoping (Month 3-4)

**Recommended pilot scope:**

- **Location:** South Kivu (Bukavu) or Katanga (Kolwezi) — established ASM zones with ITSCI presence
- **Commodity:** Tin or tantalum (clearer supply chain than gold)
- **Participants:** 2-3 ASM cooperatives, 1 negotiant (aggregator), 1 comptoir (exporter)
- **Duration:** 6 months
- **Success metric:** End-to-end digital verification from pit to first point of export, accepted by at least 1 international buyer

### Phase 4: BCC Engagement (Month 6+)

Once pilot evidence exists, approach Banque Centrale du Congo for digital certificate framework discussions. The pilot results are the credibility — not the technology pitch.

## Regulatory Landscape

| Regulation                      | Relevance                                                 | GTCX Response                                                                       |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| DRC Mining Code (2018)          | Requires traceability for ASM operators                   | Identity credentials + chain of custody verification                                |
| EU Conflict Minerals Regulation | Importers must verify responsible sourcing                | Tamper-evident digital certificates at each custody transfer                        |
| OECD Due Diligence Guidance     | 5-step framework for responsible supply chains            | Step 2 (identify risks) and Step 3 (design strategy) supported by verification data |
| Dodd-Frank Section 1502         | US-listed companies must report conflict mineral sourcing | GTCX certificates provide auditable sourcing evidence                               |

## What NOT to Say

- Do not position GTCX as replacing ITSCI — position as a digital complement
- Do not claim to solve the conflict minerals problem — claim to provide better verification tools
- Do not discuss cryptocurrency, blockchain, or tokens — use "cryptographic certificates" and "digital signatures"
- Do not promise offline capability without demonstrating it — bring a device and show it working
