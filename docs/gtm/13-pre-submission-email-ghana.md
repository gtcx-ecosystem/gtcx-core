## Email Draft

Subject: **Request for Pre-Submission Consultation — Digital Verification for Ghana's Gold and Cocoa Supply Chains**

Dear Fintech and Innovation Office,

I am writing to request a pre-submission consultation regarding the GTCX Verification Protocol — a cryptographic infrastructure for digitally verifying commodity origins, custody chains, and trade compliance.

### What We Do

GTCX provides cryptographic verification infrastructure that creates tamper-evident digital certificates for commodity lots, tracks chain of custody with digital signatures, and enables offline verification in areas without reliable connectivity. The system uses internationally audited cryptographic libraries and is FIPS 140-3 compliant via validated modules.

### Why Ghana

Ghana's commodity sectors present compelling use cases:

- **Gold** (Africa's largest producer) — digital verification from galamsey and small-scale mining operations through PMMC to export, supporting the government's formalization agenda
- **Cocoa** — EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) compliance requires verified origin data for cocoa entering the EU market. GTCX provides the cryptographic layer for traceability from farm to first point of export.
- **Oil** — custody transfer verification for petroleum products

Ghana's Fintech Regulatory Sandbox — the most established in West Africa — provides the appropriate venue for validating our protocol.

### Our Readiness

Internal security assessment complete with 9.9 million fuzz test executions (zero vulnerabilities). FIPS 140-3 via validated modules. ISO 27001, SOC 2, GDPR, PCI-DSS, SOX documentation. 21 automated quality gates on every code change.

### Our Request

A 30-minute consultation to understand sandbox admission requirements, evidence expectations, and timeline. We propose gold supply chain verification as the initial pilot scope.
